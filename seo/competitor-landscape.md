# Competitor Landscape — Forward-Looking Targeting (May 2026)

This is a planning doc, not marketing copy. Already covered (10 competitors): Buildertrend, JobTread, NEWSTAR, BuildPro, AccuLynx, Procore, CoConstruct, Contractor Foreman, Buildxact, JobNimbus.

Purpose: identify the next wave of `/<competitor>-alternative` landing pages and follow-up blog posts, ranked by fit to Cornerstone's production-home-builder positioning.

---

## HIGH priority — dedicated /alternative page within 60 days

These directly compete for production home builders or are large enough that builders comparing platforms will see them on every G2/Capterra list.

### 1. ECI Sales1440 / MarkSystems — https://www.ecisolutions.com/products/sales1440/ + /products/marksystems/
ECI's residential homebuilder suite (Sales1440 = sales/CRM for production builders, MarkSystems = ERP/PM). This is the closest direct competitor in the production-builder ERP space alongside NEWSTAR and BuildPro. Mid-to-large production builders. **Rationale:** Same segment, legacy stack, modernization angle is identical to BuildPro pitch. Likely top 3 builder-facing competitor list.

### 2. Constellation HomeBuilder Systems (BuilderMT, NEWSTAR, etc.) — https://www.constellationhb.com/
Owns NEWSTAR and BuilderMT. We have a NEWSTAR alternative page already, but a parent-brand "Constellation alternative" page captures the broader brand search and the BuilderMT product line. **Rationale:** High intent. Existing NEWSTAR work mostly transfers.

### 3. Hyphen Solutions (BuildPro, BRIX, SupplyPro) — https://www.hyphensolutions.com/
Owns BuildPro. Same story as Constellation — parent-brand capture plus BRIX (newer SaaS attempt) and SupplyPro (vendor-side). **Rationale:** Builders evaluating BuildPro often see Hyphen branding first; capturing that search alongside BuildPro is cheap.

### 4. Houzz Pro — https://www.houzz.com/pro
Houzz Pro has aggressively bundled CRM + estimating + selections + payments for residential builders and remodelers. Selections are job-level (custom-builder model), so the design-center positioning angle works directly. **Rationale:** Big brand, big SEO footprint, custom-vs-production angle is strong, captures remodeler-curious builders evaluating up-market.

### 5. Knowify — https://www.knowify.com/
QuickBooks-tight residential/commercial GC tool with budgeting and job costing. Smaller than Buildertrend but shows up on every "Buildertrend alternative" list. **Rationale:** Decent search volume on `Knowify alternative`, weak design-center story, easy positioning.

## MEDIUM priority — comparison blog post first, landing page later

Worth a 1,000-word /blog post in batch 2; revisit /alternative landing page based on traffic.

### 6. BuildBook — https://www.buildbook.co/
Custom builder + remodeler client communication tool. Lightweight, strong onboarding pitch. Same custom-vs-production angle as CoConstruct.

### 7. ServiceTitan — https://www.servicetitan.com/
Residential trades (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing) field service platform. Adjacent more than direct, but builders running trade arms or evaluating for a trade-heavy operation will compare. Position as "trades CRM, not home builder PM."

### 8. Builder360 / similar production-builder ERPs (smaller players)
Niche production-builder ERPs serving regional builders. Lower search volume but very high intent when builders find them.

### 9. WunderBuild — https://wunderbuild.com.au/
Australian-origin builder PM moving into US market. Custom-builder bias. Cleaner UI than CoConstruct. Worth one comparison post when they push US marketing harder.

### 10. STACK / PlanSwift / On-Screen Takeoff — https://www.stackct.com/, https://www.planswift.com/
Takeoff-specific tools. Cornerstone's Blueprint AI replaces these for residential floor plans. Better suited to a "AI takeoff vs traditional takeoff" comparison cluster than a head-to-head /alternative page.

## Strategic note

Cornerstone's strongest uncontested keyword cluster is **production-builder design center** terminology. Competitors don't use the phrase "Designer Packages," don't model per-floorplan options, and don't separate Structural vs Designer scope. Every batch-2 post should have one paragraph carrying that vocabulary so the topical-authority signal compounds across the cluster, not just inside individual posts.
